General

  • There are three kinds of CoxBox, the 1984 original design, the 2008 revised design and the CoxBox Mini amplifier.
  • The original CoxBox uses a BNC connector for the microphone, the 2008 CoxBox and the CoxBox Mini use a two pin Amphenol 44 series connector.
  • Original and 2008 CoxBoxes include a timing and stroke rate computer.
  • The 2008 CoxBox includes a removable battery, accessible on the bottom of the CoxBox. The battery only fits in one way around and should slot in easily and smoothly.

Basics

Before use

  • 2008 CoxBoxes only: Check the charge level on the display.
  • Unplug the charging socket. Pull from the connector, not the cable and do not twist as you pull.
  • Leave the microphone cable plugged in and take the headset off its hook.
  • Optional: Check the CoxBox amplifer by plugging in the test speaker cable, test the headset, unplug the speaker test cable.
  • Remove the CoxBox from the rack.

During use

  • Treat the CoxBox with care, don't drop it. If transporting, use a CoxBox carry case.
  • At peak times, do not take more than one CoxBox, this will deprive others of important equipment. There is no need to take multiple units, for very long outings consider taking a spare battery for the 2008 CoxBoxes.

After use

  • Switch off the CoxBox.
  • Place the CoxBox in the rack, hang the headset from a hook. Do not unplug the microphone.
  • Plug in a CoxBox charger. Find the wedge inside the socket and line it up with the top of the CoxBox. Do not twist the socket as you plug it in.
  • When properly plugged in, the CoxBox display should light up (2008) or a light should come on (original, Mini).

Troubleshooting

Any issues should be logged for investigation. See the City website (cityrc.co.uk) for more details.

  • Problem: Stroke rate meter does not work.
    Resolution: If the amplifier is working correctly, then the stroke seat may not have a magnet under the seat, the boat does not support stroke rates or the boat's wiring is at fault.
  • Problem: The amplifier / boat audio does not work or there is static.
    Resolution:
    1. Check that the CoxBox is correctly plugged in to the boat's adapter and switched on. Loose connections can cause problems.
    2. If the CoxBox is correctly connected to the boat, check that the microphone is correctly plugged in to the CoxBox. Important: do not twist the connector, remove and reconnect it.
    3. Check that the CoxBox is charged.
    4. Check the CoxBox on the test harness in the boathouse, this will isolate whether there is a problem with the CoxBox or the boat's electrics.
    5. If the problem is with the CoxBox, check that the microphone by plugging in a known working microphone. If this resolves the issue, ensure the faulty microphone is removed from service and the problem is logged.
    6. Log the fault against the CoxBox, including all the IDs of the equipment used (e.g. battery, microphone, CoxBox).
  • Problem: The audio is not heard throughout the boat.
    Resolution: Check that the CoxBox is turned up high enough, if so, check that each speaker outputs sound. If one of the speakers is not working, that is a problem with the boat and should be logged.

CoxBox identification

When reporting issues, it's important to record which pieces of equipment the problem relates to. This section explains how to identify the equipment IDs.

  • Each CoxBox will have a sticker identifying it. All IDs will start with "CB-" On original CoxBoxes, the sticker will be on the display. On 2008 and Mini CoxBoxes, the sticker will be under the rubber bumper.
  • Each microphone will have a label on the cable, near the connector and will begin with "MIC-".
  • CoxBox 2008 batteries will have an ID sticker on their side beginning with "BAT-".
  • CoxBox chargers will have an ID sticker on the side of the plug that begings with "CHRG-".
